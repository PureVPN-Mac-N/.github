## What Is PureVPN for Mac

<a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a> is a virtual private network (VPN) application for macOS that encrypts
your internet traffic and routes it through secure servers to protect your privacy and security
online. In one sentence: PureVPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your Mac and the internet,
hiding your real IP address and location while protecting your data from interception on any network.

A VPN like <a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a> serves several core purposes: it encrypts your connection
so that no one on your network — including your internet service provider, network administrators,
or attackers on public Wi-Fi — can see what you are doing online; it hides your real IP address
and replaces it with the IP of the VPN server, masking your identity and location; and it lets you
appear to be browsing from a different country by connecting to servers in other regions.

### Why Use a VPN on Your Mac

<a href="#">Mac PureVPN</a> provides protection in several important scenarios:

**Public Wi-Fi security**: Coffee shops, airports, hotels, and other public Wi-Fi networks are
insecure — traffic on them can be intercepted by others on the same network. <a href="#">PureVPN
Mac</a> encrypts your connection so that even on an untrusted public network, your data is protected.

**ISP privacy**: Your internet service provider can see every website you visit. A VPN encrypts

see you as browsing from that region, useful for accessing content available in specific locations.

## Kill Switch: Protection If the VPN Drops

<a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a> kill switch is a critical security feature that protects your privacy
if the VPN connection unexpectedly drops:

Without a kill switch, if a VPN connection drops momentarily, your Mac would automatically fall
back to the unprotected direct internet connection — exposing your real IP address and unencrypted
traffic without you realizing it. The <a href="#">PureVPN</a> kill switch prevents this: if the VPN
connection drops, the kill switch immediately blocks all internet traffic until the VPN reconnects.

This ensures that your traffic is never exposed on the unprotected connection — either the VPN is
active and protecting your traffic, or the internet is blocked. There is no window where your real
IP and unencrypted data leak. The kill switch is essential for users who need guaranteed privacy
protection.

## Split Tunneling

<a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a> split tunneling lets you choose which applications use the VPN and which
connect directly:

Split tunneling routes specific applications through the VPN while allowing others to use the direct
internet connection. This is useful when you want VPN protection for some traffic but direct
connection for others — for example, routing a web browser through the VPN for privacy while letting
a local network application or a bandwidth-heavy download connect directly for full speed.

The <a href="#">PureVPN Mac app</a> split tunneling configuration selects applications for VPN or
direct routing, giving granular control over which traffic is protected and which takes the direct path.
